History of Winnipeg

Winnipeg's history stretches from its first inhabitants, Aboriginal peoples, to the arrival of Europeans and up to the present day. In the 1700s, the first forts were built on the site of Winnipeg, followed by the Selkirk Settlement in 1812.
Winnipeg is the capital and the largest city in the province of Manitoba in Canada. It is surrounded by the Red river and Assiniboine river, near the longitudinal center of North America, and with many lakes around it, its name is due to the closest lake; the name comes from the Western Cree words for muddy water. This region was a commercial center for indigenous peoples long before the arrival of Europeans. French merchants built the first fort on the site in 1738. Subsequently, Selkirk settlers from the Red River Colony founded a settlement in 1812, the core of which was incorporated as the city of Winnipeg in 1873. As of 2016, Winnipeg is the seventh municipality Most populous in Canada, being inland, the local climate is extremely seasonal even by Canadian standards, with winter lows down to -54 ° and summer highs up to 40 ° C.

It is home to several professional sports franchises, like the Winnipeg Blue Bombers (Canadian soccer), the Winnipeg Jets (ice hockey), Manitoba Moose (ice hockey), Valor FC (soccer) and the Winnipeg Goldeyes (baseball), Winnipeg was the first Canadian host of the Pan American Games.
This multicultural city is decked out with many annual festivals, such as the Festival du Voyageur, the Winnipeg Folk Festival, the Winnipeg Jazz Festival, the Winnipeg Fringe Theater Festival and Folklorama every August, where there is food and events from every country in the world.

Geographic information

Winnipeg, also known as the 'Gateway to the West,' is located on the eastern side of the Canadian Prairies in Western Canada. The city has a total area of 464.01 km2 (179.16 sq mi) and a height of 240 m, according to the Census geographic units of Canada (790 ft).
